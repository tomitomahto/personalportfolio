The Origin

Built from
Scratch.
Twice.

No inherited network. No blueprint. No one in my corner who had done it before. I built a career in sales and a business from scratch, both without a roadmap, and that turns out to be the most useful thing I know how to do.

While working in tech, I looked around at the beauty industry and saw a problem nobody was solving. Women with darker skin tones were underserved, on shelves, on runways, in every makeup artist's kit. So I started MFMG Cosmetics with nothing but my phone and a business plan. I built a community of 300,000+ women, raised $100K in non-dilutive funding, won the Visa She's Next Grant, got accepted into NEXT Canada, and landed in the New York Times, all before the brand had a formal marketing budget.

Then I took everything I learned building something from zero and applied it inside one of the world's largest companies. $100M+ in net new revenue and five years at Google later, I am building again, this time for the people doing exactly what I did: figuring it out without anyone showing them how.

MFMG Cosmetics started as an Instagram page in 2015. I was showcasing makeup artists who worked beautifully with darker skin tones, because when I modelled, the artists on set were rarely prepared for me. "It was the first direct impact to my self-esteem," I said at the time. So I decided to fix it.

The page hit 10,000 followers in a month. A New York Times journalist reached out after one of my campaigns. Readers started asking me to create my own products. I cold-called labs using my sales background, pitched them like a client, and eventually launched the MFMG Glitter Palette, which sold out immediately.

The brand grew into a global DTC line worn by Gabrielle Union, Bozoma Saint John, India Arie, and Cynthia Erivo. I won the Visa She's Next Grant, got accepted into the NEXT Canada accelerator, was featured on Shopify Masters, and raised $100K in non-dilutive funding, all while working full-time in tech. The hashtag #MakeupForMelaninGirls has been used over one million times.

The lesson I carried into every sales leadership role after: community is the product. Build the people first. The revenue follows.
