The Origin

Built from
Scratch.
Twice.

No inherited book. No inherited network. No one in my corner who had done it before. I built a career in sales and a business from scratch, both without a roadmap, and that turns out to be the most useful thing I know how to do.

At Google, I grew into a new scope every year: 300% quota as a Noogler, 140% as a Sales Principal leading a pod of three, then Country Manager for Google Canada's acquisitions business, building a seven-person AE team from zero, compressing ramp from 5 quarters to 3, and generating $100M+ in net new revenue at 110% blended attainment.

While building in tech, I was also building outside it. I started MFMG Cosmetics with nothing but my phone and a business plan. A 300,000-person community, $100K in non-dilutive funding, coverage in the New York Times and 20+ publications, all without a PR budget. Community is the product. Build the people first. The revenue follows.

MFMG Cosmetics started as an Instagram page in 2015 , I was showcasing makeup artists who worked beautifully with darker skin tones, because when I modelled, the artists on set were rarely prepared for me. So I decided to fix it.

The page hit 10,000 followers in a month. A New York Times journalist reached out. Readers started asking me to create products. I did what I know how to do , I called the labs, pitched them like a client, and eventually launched the MFMG Glitter Palette. It sold out immediately.

The brand grew into a global DTC line featured in New York Times, Teen Vogue, Globe and Mail, Elle Canada, Shopify Masters, and 16+ other publications. Worn by Gabrielle Union, Bozoma Saint John, India Arie, and Cynthia Erivo. I won the Visa She's Next Grant, got accepted into NEXT Canada, and raised $100K in non-dilutive funding , all while holding a full-time role in tech.

The lesson that carried into every sales leadership role after: build the system, not the dependency. A team that runs without you in the room is the only team worth building.

I bring a coaching operating cadence, a ramp architecture that compresses time-to-productivity, and a consultative motion built for buyers who think in outcomes, not features. I've done it in advertising technology, bootcamp education, and DTC consumer. I'm ready to build it somewhere new.
